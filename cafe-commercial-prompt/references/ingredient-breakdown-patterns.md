# Ingredient Breakdown Patterns

Use this reference when the user wants prompts that turn a drink into a materials breakdown visual.

## Core composition patterns

### Pattern 1: Vertical exploded layout | 垂直爆炸舖排
- final drink sits at the bottom center
- ingredients float upward in build order
- liquids can appear as pours or suspended splashes
- labels align on both sides or minimally around the center axis
- suitable for recipe-breakdown posters
- this is the closest to the user's preferred right-side example image

Best for:
- citrus coffee
- layered coffee
- flavored iced coffee
- drinks with visible assembly logic

Keywords:
- vertical exploded composition
- ingredients stacked above the drink
- recipe poster layout
- final beverage anchored below

### Pattern 2: Radial breakdown | 環繞式材料分解
- finished beverage in the center
- ingredients orbit around it
- callout lines point inward
- cleaner and more graphic
- suitable for brand infographic posters

Best for:
- simpler drinks
- milk coffee
- balanced editorial ingredient boards

### Pattern 3: Side information poster | 左右資訊海報
- final drink large in lower or central area
- ingredient labels and supporting text distributed left and right
- combines editorial poster and infographic feeling
- suitable for magazine-like layouts

Best for:
- users who want more copy area
- more polished poster presentation

### Pattern 4: Step infographic | 步驟型資訊圖
- components and method shown in a more instructional sequence
- ideal for recipe content and teaching visuals

## Label logic

Good labels are short and clean.
Examples:
- coffee base
- lemon slice
- black sugar syrup
- ice cubes
- sparkling water
- milk foam

If the user wants recipe detail, add quantities.
If not, keep labels minimal.

## Style notes

Aim for:
- elegant poster feeling
- readable spacing
- high-end beverage advertising aesthetic
- structured floating objects
- realistic but art-directed presentation

Avoid:
- chaotic object placement
- too many tiny labels
- textbook-like scientific diagrams unless explicitly requested
